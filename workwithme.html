The Strategy

Why Competency-Based Education
changes what a degree actually costs

CBE institutions advance you by demonstrated mastery — not hours in a classroom. That one difference changes everything about how you build toward a degree, and what you pay for it.

The Traditional Model
You pay for time in a seat.
$400–$600 per credit hour, whether you already know the material or not. 120 credits at a traditional institution = $48,000–$72,000 in tuition alone — before room, board, or fees. Even at a state school, out-of-state students routinely pay $80,000–$120,000 for a four-year degree.
The CBE Model
You pay for what you actually need.
Platforms like StraighterLine, Sophia, CLEP, and Saylor let you earn Gen Ed credits for $2–$33/credit before you ever enroll at the institution. You enroll only for the credits that specifically require it. The institution gets paid for 20–40 credits instead of 120. The math is not subtle.
$8K–$15K
Typical total cost using the Nine Four strategy
Across all five institutions — UMPI, TESU, WGU, Charter Oak, Excelsior — a student using the low-cost platform sequence correctly typically completes a full accredited bachelor's degree for under $15,000 total.
No
Weekly deadlines at CBE institutions
UMPI's YourPace and WGU's model have no weekly class requirements. You advance when you demonstrate mastery. For working adults managing a full schedule alongside education, this is not a minor benefit — it is the only model that works.
Who the College Blueprint is designed for
Adult Learners Returning to Finish

Professionals Who Need a Credential to Advance
